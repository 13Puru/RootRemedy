Root Remedy User Manual

Welcome to the Root Remedy portal! This manual will guide you through the essential features of the platform, including how to search for medicinal plants, remedies, diseases, and how to contact the admin for consultations.

Step 1: Accessing the Portal

To start using Root Remedy, visit our portal's home page and log in as a user or browse the data directly.

  • URL: www.rootremedy.com
  • Guest Mode: You can explore the database without logging in.
  • Login: Admin users can log in with their username and password to access the admin dashboard.
Step 2: Searching for Plants, Medicines, and Diseases

The Root Remedy portal provides a search function for various categories:

  • Plants: Search for medicinal plants and their properties.
  • Medicines: Find out how to prepare medicines and the diseases they cure.
  • Diseases: Explore diseases and the remedies for them.

Use the dropdown filters for specific categories or enter a search term in the search box.

Step 3: Downloading Information

Users can download detailed reports or data about plants, medicines, or diseases as a PDF file.

  • Once you find the information you need, click the Download PDF button on the result page.
  • The downloaded PDF will contain text and images of the plant or medicine.
Step 4: Consulting with Admin

If you need expert advice on a particular medicinal plant or disease, you can contact the admin directly through the consultation form.

  • Click the Consult with Admin button in the menu.
  • Fill in your name, email, phone number (optional), and your query.
  • Click Submit to send your message.
  • The admin will review your query and respond to your email.
Step 5: Admin Dashboard (For Admins only)

If you are an admin, logging in will take you to the admin dashboard. Here you can manage plants, medicines, diseases, and consultation queries:

  • Add/Edit/Delete plants, medicines, and diseases.
  • View the number of plants, diseases, and medicines currently available in the system.
  • Respond to user consultation requests.
